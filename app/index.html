Description:

The pinnacle of Dynasty Forge's capabilities, this classic Samurai O-Katana (extended length sword) is made completely in the historical method. Starting with small batch low-temperature smelted steel made from iron sand, our smiths pick individual pieces to balance the carbon content of the blade.

The blade is then forged and folded to remove impurities from the Tamahagane, Clay-tempered and then hand polished over 40 hours on Japanese natural stones, the polish allows the wielder to see deeply into the Hada and Hamon. Matched with darkened silver Buddhist prayer wheel fittings and Japanese silk Ito, this sword is the centerpiece of any collection.

Our Features

Smelting

Smelters of tamahagane today make it the same way it was made in ancient times. It is made in a type if furnace called a tatara. As the smelting process begins, the tatara is slowly filled with charcoal and iron sand. This process can take days.

Heat Treating

The differentially hardened blade is part of the sword's ability to hold a sharp edge while maintaining blade flexibility. The sword smith packs the spine of the blade with a special clay during tempering. It acts as an insulation allowing the spine to cool slower then the edge.

Folding

In traditional Japanese sword making, the low-carbon iron is folded several times by itself, to purify it. This produces the soft metal to be used for the core of the blade. The high-carbon steel and the higher-carbon cast-iron are then forged in alternating layers.

Polishing
